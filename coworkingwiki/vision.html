The Vision

“It’s no surprise that the wiki was built from the ground up by avid contributors to the open source movement. They laid the groundwork for thousands of people to share what they have learned, because they believe in the greater value that stems from open collaboration.”
Will Bennis, Locus Workspace

Jacob Sayles and Will Bennis have maintained the coworking wiki for a few years, and as volunteers, only have time to prevent spam and approve new users. This system isn't able to keep up with the growth of the movement and we believe we can do so much more.

Help us build the movement together.

The improved wiki will be an effective platform to:

  • Collaborate: We want to share our experiences in a friendly, and accessible way. By combining the lessons we learn in our local communities, we can all share the benefits in this global community.

  • Educate: When someone new comes to the wiki we want them to be able to easily understand everything and ask questions when they need help finding something specific. When conversations come up on the google group, we'll be able to connect it to the content on the wiki.

  • Discover: We’ll have maps! We've never been able to truly map out the spaces listed because the data isn't machine readable. We've tried a few times to create a better system but the different approaches have either not been open or not sustainable as we grow. The Open Coworking Directory changes this by pulling meta-data from each space's website and is extensible in a wide variety of ways. We'll be able to embed the data in the wiki itself, as well as in other contexts like coworkingseattle.org.
